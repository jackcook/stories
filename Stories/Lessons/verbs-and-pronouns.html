Verbs and Pronouns

  • A verb is a word that tells you what someone does: eat, sleep, work, love

  • A pronoun is a word that replaces a person or a thing: I, you, he, she ...

  • When we combine pronouns and verbs, we basically only allow certain endings, e.g. I drink — he drinks

je I
tu you (informal singular)
il he
elle she
nous we
vous you (plural, formal singular)
ils they (males)
elles they (females)

  • Unlike English, French has two words for "you": "vous" and "tu". It is important to know when to use each one.
  • "Vous" is the plural form of "you". It is used to show respect.
    • Note: Use it when you are talking to someone important, someone who is older than you, or someone you are unfamiliar with.
  • "Tu" is the singular and more informal form of "you".
    • Note: Use it when you are referring to a friend or family member, or when addressing a child and with someone who you would call by his/her first name.
Note: There is no "it" form in French. Everything is either masculine or feminine, and therefore referred to as "il" or "elle".
