Formal Speech

Unlike English, French has two words for you, and these are vous and tu. It is important to know when to use each one. Vous is the plural form of you. This is somewhat equivalent to you all, and it can be used in both informal and formal circumstances. It can also be used to refer to one person to show respect. This could be when talking to someone important, someone who is older than you, or someone you are unfamiliar with.
Tu, on the other hand, is the singular and more informal form of you. In French, it is commonly used when referring to a friend or family member, or when addressing a child. If you use tu while talking to a stranger, it shows disrespect. As a rule of thumb, use tu with someone who you would call by his/her first name, otherwise use vous.
